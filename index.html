About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher working on machine learning for medical imaging at Martinos Center, hosted by Matthew Rosen and Juan Eugenio Iglesias.

Before coming to Boston, I worked on medical image analysis with deep learning and got a Ph.D. degree (Summa Cum Laude) from Department of Computer Science at TUM, advised by Bjoern Menze. During the Ph.D., I visited Daniel Rueckert's lab and Department of Quantitative Biomedicine at UZH for two years.

During the Master program at Sun Yat-sen University, I was jointly supervised by Wei-Shi Zheng and Jianguo Zhang on computer vision and medical image analysis. As a student, I visited School of Computing at the University of Dundee for eight months, at the end of which, we won the WMH segmentation challenge at MICCAI 2017.

At the beginning of my research, I worked with Wing-Kuen Ling on optimization for retinal image analysis for my Bachelor thesis.
